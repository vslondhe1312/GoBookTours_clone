Is India safe to travel?

While India is considered safe to travel than many of the Western Countries, but we suggest you to take presonal precautions for safety purpose. India's rich tapestry of vibrant culture, breathtaking landscapes, and spiritual havens beckon, responsible travel is key. What is the best time to visit India?

The best time to visit India is typically from October to March when the weather is pleasant and conducive to exploration. During these months, many popular destinations such as Rajasthan, Delhi, Agra, Kerala, and Goa experience cooler temperatures, making it ideal for sightseeing and outdoor activities. Summer, from April to June, can be hot, but hill stations like Shimla and Darjeeling offer respite. The monsoon season, from July to September, brings heavy rainfall, creating lush landscapes, particularly in regions like the Western Ghats. While the monsoons can enhance the natural beauty, it's essential to consider potential travel disruptions. Therefore, planning your visit based on your preferred destinations and the experiences you seek will ensure a more enjoyable trip to this diverse and vibrant country.

What is Golden Triangle India?

The Golden Triangle is a popular tourist circuit in India that connects the national capital, New Delhi, with the city of Agra, home to the Taj Mahal, and the city of Jaipur, the capital of Rajasthan. The Golden Triangle is so named because of the triangular shape formed by the locations of these three cities on a map.

What are some of the best destinations to visit in India for Foreign tourists?

India boasts a plethora of captivating destinations that are a treasure trove for foreign tourists. Among these, Jaipur in Rajasthan stands out as the "Pink City, " renowned for its historic forts, palaces, and bustling markets, with must-visit attractions like the City Palace, Hawa Mahal, and Amer Fort. Agra, in Uttar Pradesh, is home to the iconic Taj Mahal, alongside the impressive Agra Fort and Fatehpur Sikri, showcasing India's rich architectural heritage. Varanasi, also in Uttar Pradesh, is one of the world's oldest continuously inhabited cities, offering spiritual experiences, ancient temples, and the mesmerizing Ganga Aarti ceremony along the banks of the Ganges River. Goa, with its beautiful beaches and vibrant nightlife, is a popular destination for relaxation and cultural exploration. Further south, Kerala's serene backwaters, lush landscapes, and Ayurvedic treatments make it a unique and rejuvenating experience. Rishikesh and Haridwar in Uttarakhand are twin cities on the Ganges, known for their spiritual significance and adventure activities like white-water rafting. Delhi, the capital city, seamlessly blends historic landmarks with modern attractions and diverse culinary experiences. Mumbai, the financial capital, offers a dynamic urban experience with iconic landmarks like the Gateway of India and Marine Drive. Khajuraho in Madhya Pradesh boasts intricately carved temples, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, while Hampi in Karnataka fascinates with ancient ruins and a distinctive landscape, appealing to history enthusiasts and those seeking an offbeat travel experience. When exploring these destinations, it's essential to consider travel advisories and plan the itinerary based on personal interests and the time of year to maximize the India experience.

What are some of the most popular Indian national parks?

Some of the most popular national parks in India are: Ranthambore National Park, Bandhavgarh, Tadoba-Andhari National Park, Kanha National Park, Pench National Park, Panna National Park, Gir National Park, Bandipur National Park, Nagarhole, Kaziranga, Sundarbans Mangrove Forest, Hemis National Park, and Jim Corbett National Park.

What is the visa process for India?

VISA process in India, depends on your passport issuing country generally your home country. Indian Tourist Visa on arrival is available for 11 countries, i.e, Finland, Japan, Luxembourg, New Zealand, Singapore, Cambodia, Vietnam, Philippines, Laos, Indonesia, and Myanmar. Tourist from other countries can apply for VISA from their Indian Embassy located in their home country.
